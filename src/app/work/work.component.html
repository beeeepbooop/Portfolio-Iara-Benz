bachelor/game design: was wir nicht sehen

Mental illnesses can be hidden, complex, and uncomfortable. However, in order to break stigmas and create a positive world, it is important to face them head-on. This led to "Was wir nicht sehen" (transl. what we don't see), a concept for a video game that was designed as part of my bachelor's thesis. It tackles the topic of depression by letting players step into the mind of a person suffering from depression. The players guide the character through her negative feelings and help her in overcoming her struggles. This game playfully addresses mental health and the importance of reducing biases.

ui/ux design: "bodily"

"Bodily" is an app concept that aims to solve the problems that come with online shopping. This app aims to revolutionize the online shopping industry by making it smarter, simpler, and more sustainable. Through the use of Body-ID‘s consumers can instantly know whether an item of clothing from third-party retailers will fit themselves and their friends, thus avoiding the time consuming and environmentally damaging process of constantly returning ill-fitting clothing. The app design is characterized by simple shapes and vibrant characters, creating a user-friendly interface and brand.

illustration:
das kleinste
von allem

„Das Kleinste von Allem“ (transl. The Smallest of Everything) is a lighthearted children's book about a tiny spider who begins a journey into the vast world around itself in search of anything that is even smaller than itself. In the short book the spider overcomes new challenges, allowing itself to gain confidence and new perspectives. This story book should encourage its young readers to be brave, choose their own path and enjoy their differences. The book design is simple and effective through its limited color scheme and the use of adorable characters. Also the hand-lettered text is easy to read by young children.

animation: "bun's adventures"

"Bun's Adventures!" is a concept for an animated children's show about the adventurous little bunny "Bun", who is always up to new and exciting antics with his friends. Shown here is a hand-animated intro scene for the show, that gives viewers insights into Bun's life and the show. In order to not be too visually stimulating for its young viewers the show has been deliberately kept simple, with easily recognizable characters, limited colors and the use of empty space.

ad design: 100th anniversary

for their 100th anniversary the shoe manufacturing company LOWA was looking for a new ad campaign. this potential concept was created to celebrate the longstanding company and all the memories that their loyal customers had with their shoes. this creative design mixes the impression such memories can make with the actual shoe imprints in order to create a brand new striking ad campaign.

interior design: drees & sommer

In the scope of a student competition, me and my two teammates, were tasked with creating a communicative design for the new Drees & Sommer building, that appeals both to the daily workers and guests. In our approach we focused on presenting the mission statements of the company through the use of interactive columns, innovative light and appealing typography. Our design ultimately won third place in the competition.

corporate design: höfen
an der enz

The local municipality of “Höfen an der Enz”, in the black forest, was searching for a brand new identity that would freshen up their public image and breathe new life into their tranquil community. In this concept, all significant aspects of the area were simplified into colorful shapes and utilized alongside lively typography and playful photography in order to bring a more fun, youthful and family-oriented flair to this small town.

magazine design: "verschwinden"

In this experimental magazine images and texts become intertwined and form new meanings. With this abstract form of storytelling, the emotional concept of “Verschwinden” (transl. disappearing) is analyzed and documented through the use of exploratory typography and photography.

magazine & font design: "virus"

Using my selfmade monotype font "Virus", this magazine visualizes an experimental exploration of computer viruses through distorted typography and composition. The magazine itself depicts what it means to have been ravaged by an unknown and invisible computer virus, as shown through the severe distortions and eligibility of the pages and texts.
