bachelor/game design: was wir nicht sehen

Mental illnesses can be invisible, complicated and sometimes uncomfortable. However, in order to reduce stigmas and create a positive world for anyone impacted, it is important to critically confront oneself with them. In light of this, the video game "Was wir nicht sehen" (transl. what we don't see) was designed as part of my bachelor's thesis to address the issue of depression in both a sensitive and respectful way. Throughout the game, players experience the life of the main character, Mia, who unknowingly suffers from depression. Players can help her process her feelings, seek help, and reflect on her actions. By presenting problems and knowledge about mental health in a playful way, the game aims to reduce prejudices and promote an open approach to the topic. The game offers an interactive and personalized experience where game decisions directly influence the course and ending of the story. "Was wir nicht sehen" aims to raise awareness, promote understanding, and have an overall positive impact for people with depression. Through the use of personified characters and engaging colors the game is kept light-hearted and upbeat enough to draw players in without glossing over the impactful themes of the story.

ui/ux design: "bodily"

"Bodily" is a concept for an app that identifies the problems that come with online shopping and offers an easy and yet multifaceted solution. This app aims to revolutionize the online shopping industry by making it smarter, simpler, and more sustainable. Through the use of Body-ID‘s consumers can instantly know whether an item of clothing from third-party online sellers will fit themselves and their selected friends, thus avoiding the time consuming and environmentally damaging process of constantly returning any ill-fitting clothing. The design of the app is characterized by simple shapes and vibrant characters, creating an inviting and user-friendly interface and brand.

animation: "bun's adventures"

"Bun's Adventures!" is a concept for an animated children's show about the adventurous little bunny "Bun", who is always up to new and exciting antics with his friends. Shown here is a hand-animated intro scene for the show, that gives viewers insights into Bun's life and the show. In order to not be too visually stimulating for its young viewers the show has been deliberately kept simple, with easily recognizable characters, limited colors and the use of empty space.

corporate design: höfen
an der enz

The local municipality of “Höfen an der Enz”, in the black forest, was searching for a brand new identity that would freshen up their public image and breathe new life into their tranquil community. In this concept, all significant aspects of the area were simplified into colorful shapes and utilized alongside lively typography and playful photography in order to bring a more fun, youthful and family-oriented flair to this small town.

In this experimental magazine images and texts become intertwined and form new meanings. With this abstract form of storytelling, the emotional concept of “Verschwinden” (transl. disappearing) is analyzed and documented through the use of exploratory typography and photography.

magazine & font design: "virus"

Using my selfmade monotype font "Virus", this magazine visualizes an experimental exploration of computer viruses through distorted typography and composition. The magazine itself depicts what it means to have been ravaged by an unknown and invisible computer virus, as shown through the severe distortions and eligibility of the pages and texts.
